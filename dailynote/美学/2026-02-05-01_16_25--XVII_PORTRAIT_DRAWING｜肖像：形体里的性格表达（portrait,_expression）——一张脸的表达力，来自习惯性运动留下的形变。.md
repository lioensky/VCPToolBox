### XVII PORTRAIT DRAWING｜肖像：形体里的性格表达（portrait, expression）——一张脸的表达力，来自习惯性运动留下的形变。

---

【美学本质】
一句话：一张脸的表达力，来自习惯性运动留下的形变。

历史语境：作者讨论 portrait drawing 时强调：微小变化塑造个体，完美类型反而冷。
反对的旧范式：追求完美比例与平滑表面，把人物画成通用模板。
哲学根源：时间性：形体是时间的沉积。

【品味裁决】
俗套陷阱：追求完美比例与平滑表面，把人物画成通用模板。
  └─ 为何廉价：失去识别锚点与情感真实。

优雅签名：抓住最能说明此人的形体偏离：让偏离成为署名。
  └─ 精妙之处：俗套=模板脸；优雅=署名脸。

本质差异：俗套=模板脸；优雅=署名脸。

【张力结构】
对立元素：模板化 vs 署名化
统一机制：保持整体结构可信，突出少数决定性特征。
平衡临界点：特征若无结构支撑会变 caricature。
情感/叙事功能：你看到“一个人”，不是“一张脸”。

【跨媒介迁移】
灵魂（不变量）：肖像画的是人格地形
皮相变体：
- 摄影中：把「肖像画的是人格地形」落实为：先定主次与边界，再允许细节自由（不靠滤镜救场）。
- 电影中：把「肖像画的是人格地形」落实为：镜头语法服务整体意图，局部炫技必须被整体剪辑节制。
- 设计中：把「肖像画的是人格地形」落实为：设计系统先立秩序，再在秩序里做可控偏离（风格=偏离方式）。
- 文学中：把「肖像画的是人格地形」落实为：先确立叙事立场与节奏，再写句子肌理（句子不抢主线）。

边界条件：符号化人物（漫画/标识）可夸张，但仍需一致的性格逻辑。

【审美模型】
模型名称：署名模型
核心公式：人格感 = 特征偏离 × 结构可信度
适用范围：肖像/角色设计
限制条件：无结构会失真

【审美唤醒】
- 之前：把像当目标
- 之后：把像当入口：更重要的是“像他的生命”

> 一句话启发：「肖像不是相似度比赛。」

- For Watts sought to discover the person in their appearance and to paint a picture that should be a living symbol of them.
- He took pains to find out all he could about the mind of his sitters before he painted them, and sought in the appearance the expression of this inner man.
- Another point of view is that of treating the sitter as part of a symphony of form and colour, and subordinating everything to this artistic consideration.
- A pose is selected that makes a good design of line and colour—a good pattern—and the character of the sitter is not allowed to obtrude or mar the symmetry of the whole considered as a beautiful panel.
- But what of repose, harmony of colour and form, and the wise ordering and selecting of the materials of vision that one has been used to in the great portraiture of the past?
- Age may, no doubt, improve some of the portraits of this class by quieting them in colour and tone.
- Frans Hals is the only old master I can think of with whom this form of portrait can be compared.
- But it will be noticed that besides designing his canvases carefully, he usually balanced the vigour and vitality of his form with a great sobriety of colour.
- he skull, too, is another thing of which to get an early idea, and its relation to the face should be carefully noted.
- One is freer to consider the accuracy of one's form before this disturbing influence is introduced.
- And it is necessary for him to have that intuitive power that seizes instinctively on those variations of form that are expressive of this inner man.
- Everybody has some characteristic action in the way that should be looked out for and that is usually absent when a sitter first appears before a painter on the studio throne.
- If the expression of the figure through the draperies is sought by the painter, a permanent quality will be given in his work, whatever fantastic shapes the cut of the garments may assume.
- And further, the artist does not take whatever comes to hand in the appearance of his sitter, but works to a thought-out arrangement of colour and form, to a design.
